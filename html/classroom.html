  • Newton's Law of Gravitation

    It states that any two particles on the universe attract each other with a force which is directly proportional to the product of their masses and inversely proportional to the square of the distance between them.

    If the distance between two particles of masses M1 and M2 be r, the gravitational force (F) is given by :
    F $\propto \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2}$
    or, F = $\frac{G m_1 m_2}{r^2}$

    The constant of proportionality (G) is gravitational constant and its value G = 6.67 $\times 10^{-11} Nm^2kg^{-2}$. This constant has the same value everywhere in the universe hance G is also called universal gravitational constant. The dimensions of G $ [M^{-1}L^3T^{-2}]$
    It should be noted that gravitational force between two particle doesn't depends on presence of other particles and property of intervening medium.

  • Acceleration due to gravity (g)

    The acceleration produced in a body by the force due to gravity is called acceleration due to gravity and is denoted by g.
    If m be the mass of a body. Force due to gravity (F) is given by F = mg.
    Let M be the mass of earth and R be its radius the force on a body of mass m at earth surface is
    F = $\frac{GMm}{R^2}$
    or,$mg = \frac{GMm}{R^2}$
    or, $g = \frac{GM}{R^2}$
    The above expression shows that value of g depends on the distance of body from the centre of earth. If r be the distance of the body from the centre of earth.
    $ g' = \frac{GM}{r^2} $

  • Variation of g

    a) With altitude (height)
    Let acceleration due to gravity at earth surface and at height (h) from earth surface be g and g' respectively. Then
    $g = \frac{GM}{R^2}$
    and, $g' = \frac{GM}{(R+h)^2}$
    Dividing,
    $\frac{g'}{g} = \frac{R^2}{(R+h)^2}$
    if h << R,
    $g' = g(1-\frac{2h}{R})$
    b) With depth (d)
    Let us assume the earth has uniform density ($\rho$). then,
    Mass = Volume $\times$ density
    or $M = \frac{4}{3}\pi r^3 \times \rho$
    or $M = \frac{4}{3}\pi r^3 \rho$
    then, $ g = \frac{GM}{r^2}$
    or, $ g = \frac{G}{r^2}\times\frac{4}{3}\pi r^3 \rho$
    or, $g = \frac{4}{3} \pi G r \rho$
    hence inside earth, gvaries directly with r (distance from center or earth)
    Let g' be the acceleration due to gravity at a depth d. then
    $\frac{g'}{g} = \frac{R-d}{R}$
    or, $g' = g(1 - \frac{d}{R})$
    This equation shows the variation of g with depth.
    Weight of body at the center of earth at the center of earth d = R
    $g' = g(1 - \frac{R}{R})$
    or, $g' = g \times 0)
    = 0$
    Hence, weight of body at the center of the earth is zero.
    c) Variation of g with the rotation of earth
    Due to rotation of earth, the effective value of g decreases. The effective value of acceleration due to gravity at any latitude of angle $ \theta $ is
    $ g' = g - \omega ^2 R cos^2\theta $
    $= g - (\frac{2\pi}{T})^2Rcos^2\theta $
    where T is the time period of rotation of earth (T=24 hour).
    The effective weight of mass m is
    $ w = mg - m\omega ^2 R cos^2\theta $
    At pole,
    $ \theta = 90 ^o $ thus $ g' = g $
    At equator,
    $ \theta = 0^ o, \therefore cos\theta =1 $
    Therefore, the decrease in g is maximum at equator and zero at poles. Change in the weight of body when taken from equation to pole $ = m \omega ^2 R $
    d) Variation of g due to shape of the earth
    The earth is bulging at the equator and flattened at the poles. As varies inversely with square of radius, the value of g increases from equator to poles. ie g is maximum at pole and minimum at the euator.

  • Gravitational field strength

    The gravitation field strength at any point in gravitational field is the gravitational force experienced by a unit mass placed at that point.
    $ \vec{E} = \frac{\vec{F}}{m} = \frac{GMm}{r^2m} = \frac{GM}{r^2} $
    Gravitational field strength is a vector quantity whose dimensions are same as acceleration.

  • Gravitational potential

    Gravitational potential at any point in a gravitational field is defined as the work done in taking a unit mass from infinity to that point. The gravitation potential due to mass M at a distance r i given by.
    $V = -\frac{GM}{r}$

  • Gravitational potential energy

    The gravitational potential energy of a system of masses is defined as the amount of work done to assemble this system of masses by bringing them in from an infinite distance. The gravitational potential energy of mass M at the distance r from centre of earth is
    $ U = -\frac{GMm}{r} $

  • Motion of satellite

    Consider a satellite of mass m revolving round the earth at the radius r with velocity $V_o$. The necessary centripetal force is provided by the gravitational force between satellite and earth.
    $\frac{mV_o}{r} = \frac{GMm}{r^2}$
    or, $V_o = \sqrt{\frac{GM}{r}} $
    If the satellite is at the height h from earth surface.
    $ V_o = \sqrt{\frac{GM}{R+h}} $
    We have, $ GM = gR^2 $
    $ V_o = R\sqrt{\frac{g}{R+h}} $
    The satellite is near to earth surface $ R + h \approx R $, Hence, orbital velocity of such satellite is given by
    $ V_o = \sqrt{gR} $
    $\approx 8km/sec $

  • Time period (T) of a satellite

    $ T = \frac{2\pi r}{V_o} $
    or, $T = \frac{2 \pi r}{\sqrt{GM/r}}$
    or, $ T^2 = \frac{4 \pi^2r^3}{GM}$
    or $ T^2 = (\frac{4 \pi^2}{GM})r^3 $
    $ \therefore T^2 \propto r^3 $
    The above relation suggests that square of time period of satellite is directly proportional to the cube of the ratious of the orbit. This is infact kepler's III law. Now, lets talk about the energy of the satellite, obviously, satellite in its orbit has both kinetic and potential energy. We will shortly see the magnitudes of such energies and their relationship.

  • Kinetic and potential energy of satellite

    $E_k = \frac{1}{2}mV_o = \frac{GMm}{2(R+h)}$
    Potential energy of satellite
    $V = \frac{-GMm}{R+h}$
    Total energy of satellite
    $E_r = $ Kinetic energy + potential energy
    $ = \frac{GMm}{2(R+h)} -\frac{GMm}{(R+h)}$
    $ = -\frac{GMm}{2(R+h)} $
    The total energy of satellite is found to be negative, the negative sign indicates satellite is bound.
    From above, following relationship can easily be deduced.
    $2E_k = -U$
    $E_k = -E_r $
    $U = 2E_r$

  • Escape Velocity

    It is the least velocity with which a body must be projected upward so that it may just escape the gravitational field of the earth.
    Abody can escape from gravitation pull, if energy equal to its potential energy provided in the form of kinetic energy.
    $\frac{1}{2}mv_e^2 = \frac{GMm}{R}$
    or, $v_e = \sqrt{\frac{2GM}{R}}$
    $ = \sqrt{2gR} $
    = 11.2km/s
    Atmosphere on a planet can exist if the root mean square velocity of gas molecules less than escape velocity of that planet.

  • Geo Stationary satellite

    Followings are the essential features of geo stationary satellites.
    - Time period is same as time taken by earth to complete one rotation around own axis (T=24 hour).
    - It revolves round the earth in west to east direction in equatorial plane at a height of about 36,000km.
    - Its orbital velocity is nearly 3.1 km/sec.
    - Geo stationary satellite appears to be fixed at position above a certain place on the earth.
