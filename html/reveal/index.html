Newton's Law of Gravitation

"Any two particles on the universe attract each other with a force which is directly proportional to the product of their masses and inversely proportional to the square of the distance between them."

F $\propto \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2}$
or, F = $\frac{G m_1 m_2}{r^2}$
where, $G = 6.67 \times 10^-11 N m^2 kg^-2$

Acceleration due to gravity (g)

$ g' = \frac{GM}{r^2} $

Derivations

F = $\frac{GMm}{R^2}$
or, $mg = \frac{GMm}{R^2}$
or, $g = \frac{GM}{R^2}$

Variation of g with

altitude (height)

For, h << R,
$g' = g(1-\frac{2h}{R})$

depth (d)

$g' = g(1 - \frac{d}{R})$

the rotation of earth

$ g' = g - \omega ^2 R cos^2\theta $
